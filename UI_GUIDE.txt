PORTFOLIO OPTIMIZER - UI GUIDE
==============================
A walkthrough of every element in the dashboard, tab by tab.


OVERVIEW
--------
When you open the app, you see:

  1. Sidebar        - all your controls (stock selection, parameters, buttons)
  2. Header bar     - shows exchange, number of securities selected, live badge
  3. Ticker strip   - scrollable bar showing live price and change % for every selected stock
  4. 6 tabs         - the main content area


SIDEBAR
-------
The sidebar is the control panel. Everything you configure here affects the entire app.

  Live Auto-Refresh toggle
      Turns auto-refresh on or off.

  Refresh Every
      How often the live data updates. Options: 5s, 10s, 15s, 30s, 60s.

  Exchange
      Switch between NSE and BSE.

  Preset Basket
      One-click selection of a themed group of stocks.
      Options: Tata Group, Top IT, Top Banking, Top Auto, Pharma, Energy.

  Select Companies
      Search and pick any companies from the full 130+ stock list.
      Changing this selection automatically triggers a fresh data fetch.

  Lookback Period
      How far back historical data goes. Options: 6 months to 5 years.

  Optimization Strategy
      Which strategy to use in the Optimizer tab.
      Options: Max Sharpe, Min Volatility, Risk Parity, Max Diversification, Equal Weight.

  Risk-Free Rate (%)
      Used for Sharpe and Sortino calculations. Defaults to 6.5% (Indian 10-yr G-Sec).

  Stock to Predict
      Which stock to run the prediction model on.

  Model
      Which prediction model to use.
      Options: ARIMA, Linear Regression, Random Forest, Monte Carlo, EMA Trend.

  Forecast Horizon (Days)
      How many trading days ahead to forecast. Range: 5 to 180 days.

  Run Analysis button
      Fetches historical price data and runs all risk and optimisation calculations.

  Run Prediction button
      Runs the selected prediction model and shows the forecast chart.


TICKER STRIP
------------
The horizontal scrollable bar just below the page header. Updates on every refresh cycle.

  Format: TICKER  Price  Change%
  Green  = stock is up today
  Red    = stock is down today
  Gray   = no change or data not yet loaded


TAB 1 - LIVE FEED
-----------------
The main live monitoring screen. Shows real-time market data for all selected stocks.

  Stock Cards
      Each stock gets its own card arranged in a 3-column grid. Each card shows:

        Company name and ticker symbol
        Current price in rupees
        Day's change in % with up/down arrow (green = up, red = down)
        52-Week High  - highest price in the past 52 weeks
        52-Week Low   - lowest price in the past 52 weeks
        Volume        - 3-month average trading volume in Lakhs
        Market Cap    - market capitalisation in rupees Billions

  Market Breadth
      A panel showing how many of your selected stocks are:
        Gainers - up today, shown in green with a progress bar
        Losers  - down today, shown in red
        Flat    - no change, shown in gray
      The bar fill shows what percentage of your portfolio is in each bucket.

  Daily Change % Chart
      A bar chart showing today's percentage change for every selected stock,
      sorted from biggest loser to biggest gainer.
      Green bars = up, Red bars = down.

  Benchmark Performance
      A line chart comparing cumulative returns of Nifty 50 and Sensex over your
      chosen lookback period. Useful as a reference against your own portfolio.


TAB 2 - CHARTS
--------------
Detailed price and technical analysis for one stock at a time.
Use the dropdown at the top to switch between stocks.

  Candlestick Chart
      Shows Open, High, Low, Close price bars for each trading day.
        Green candle  = price closed higher than it opened
        Red candle    = price closed lower than it opened
        MA20          = 20-day moving average, tracks short-term trend (amber dotted line)
        MA50          = 50-day moving average, tracks medium-term trend (purple dotted line)
        MA200         = 200-day moving average, tracks long-term trend (pink dotted line)
        Bollinger Bands = +/- 2 standard deviations from MA20 (faint blue shading)
                          Price near upper band = overbought
                          Price near lower band = oversold

  Volume Chart
      Bar chart below the candlestick showing daily traded volume.
      Green = up day, Red = down day.

  RSI - Relative Strength Index
      A momentum oscillator ranging from 0 to 100.
        Above 70 = overbought, stock may be due for a pullback (red zone)
        Below 30 = oversold, stock may be due for a bounce (green zone)
        30 to 70 = neutral zone

  MACD - Moving Average Convergence Divergence
      A trend-following momentum indicator.
        MACD line      = difference between 12-day and 26-day EMA (purple line)
        Signal line    = 9-day EMA of MACD (amber line)
        Histogram bars = difference between MACD and Signal
                         Green bars = bullish momentum
                         Red bars   = bearish momentum
        When MACD crosses above Signal = potential buy signal
        When MACD crosses below Signal = potential sell signal

  Normalised Performance (Base 100)
      All selected stocks plotted together starting from a common base of 100.
      Shows which stocks have outperformed or underperformed each other.

  Correlation Matrix
      A heatmap showing how pairs of stocks move together.
        Close to +1.0 = stocks move together strongly (dark green)
        Close to -1.0 = stocks move in opposite directions (dark red)
        Close to  0   = little relationship between the two stocks
      Lower correlation between stocks = better portfolio diversification.


TAB 3 - RISK
------------
Quantitative risk analysis for each stock individually.

  Risk Metrics Table
      One row per stock with the following columns:

        Ann Ret %   Annualised compound return over the lookback period
        Ann Vol %   Annualised volatility. Higher value = more risky
        Sharpe      Return earned per unit of risk. Above 1.0 is considered good
        Max DD %    Maximum Drawdown - worst peak-to-trough decline in the period
        VaR 95%     Value at Risk at 95% confidence.
                    On the worst 5% of days, loss exceeds this percentage
        CVaR 95%    Conditional VaR - average loss on those worst 5% of days.
                    Always worse than VaR

      The table uses colour gradients. Green shading = better value, Red shading = worse value.

  Rolling 21-Day Volatility Chart
      A line chart showing how each stock's risk has changed over time.
      Spikes indicate periods of high uncertainty such as market crashes or earnings events.
      Useful to check if a stock is currently more or less volatile than its historical average.

  Drawdown Chart
      Select a stock from the dropdown. The chart shows the running drawdown,
      which is how far the price is below its previous peak at any point in time.
      The deeper the red area, the worse the loss was at that moment.


TAB 4 - OPTIMIZER
-----------------
Portfolio construction using mathematical optimisation.
Finds the best way to allocate money across your selected stocks.

  Key Metrics (4 stat cards at the top)

    Annual Return    Expected compounded return if the optimised weights were held
    Annual Volatility Expected risk level of the portfolio
    Sharpe Ratio     Risk-adjusted return of the optimised portfolio
    Max Drawdown     Worst historical drawdown at these weights

  Allocation Pie Chart
      Shows the optimal weight for each stock as a percentage of the total portfolio.
      Stocks with a weight below 0.01% are hidden.

  Weight Allocation Bar Chart
      A horizontal bar chart of the same weights, sorted from highest to lowest.
      The darker the bar, the higher the allocation.

  Strategy Comparison Table
      Runs all 5 strategies simultaneously and shows their metrics side by side.

        Max Sharpe         Maximises risk-adjusted return (best return per unit of risk)
        Min Volatility     Minimises portfolio variance (least risky combination)
        Risk Parity        Each stock contributes equally to total portfolio risk
        Max Diversification Maximises the diversification ratio across all assets
        Equal Weight       Same weight to every stock, a simple 1/N baseline

      Green highlights = best value in that column.


TAB 5 - SCORECARD
-----------------
A risk rating for each individual stock, designed for quick comparison.

  Score Cards
      Each stock gets a card showing:
        Score (0-100)   Composite risk score based on return, volatility, Sharpe, drawdown and VaR
        Risk Level      Low / Moderate / High / Very High
        Progress bar    Colour-coded bar. Green = low risk, Red = high risk

      Lower score = lower risk. Higher score = higher risk.

  Risk Radar Chart
      A spider chart where each axis represents a different risk metric:
        Ann. Return %
        Ann. Volatility %
        Sharpe Ratio
        Max Drawdown %
        Hist VaR 95%

      Each stock gets its own coloured polygon on the chart.
      A stock with a large polygon on Volatility and VaR but small on Return = high risk, low reward.
      Ideal stocks have large Return/Sharpe polygons and small Volatility/Drawdown polygons.

  Full Metrics Table
      The complete numerical scorecard for all stocks in one table,
      including every individual metric used to calculate the composite score.


TAB 6 - PREDICT
---------------
Machine learning and statistical price forecasting for a single stock.
Configure the stock, model and horizon in the sidebar, then click Run Prediction.

  Model Info Banner
      Shows the selected model, stock name, forecast horizon, and a brief description
      of what the model does.

  Forecast Chart
      A price chart split into two sections:
        Historical prices  Last 180 days of actual prices (dark navy line)
        Forecast           Predicted prices for the next N days (purple dashed line)
        Confidence band    Range of likely outcomes (purple shaded area)
                           A wider band means more uncertainty in the forecast
        Today line         Vertical dotted line marking where history ends and forecast begins

  Key Metrics (4 stat cards)

    Current Price    Last known price
    In N Days        Forecasted price at the end of the horizon with % change
    RMSE             Root Mean Squared Error - average prediction error in rupees. Lower = more accurate
    MAPE             Mean Absolute Percentage Error - average % error. Lower = more accurate

  Scenario Analysis Table
      Shows the forecast at three time points:
        1 Week    Price in approximately 5 trading days
        1 Month   Price in approximately 20 trading days
        N Days    Price at the end of your chosen horizon

      Each row also shows the expected % change from today and a BUY or SELL signal
      based on whether the forecast is above or below the current price.

  Prediction Models

    ARIMA              Best for short-term forecasts on stocks with clear time-series patterns
    Linear Regression  Best for trend-based stocks. Uses lag features and technical indicators
    Random Forest      Best for non-linear patterns. More robust to noisy data
    Monte Carlo (GBM)  Best for probability-based range forecasts. Shows distribution of possible futures
    EMA Trend          Simple directional forecast. Extrapolates the current moving average trend

  Note: These forecasts are statistical estimates, not financial advice.
        Use them alongside your own research.

  Amber  (#F59E0B)  Warning, moderate risk, signal line
